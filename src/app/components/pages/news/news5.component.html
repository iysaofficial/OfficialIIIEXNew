Raising the Issue of "Pecalang Segara", Undiksha Team Wins Silver Medal at IIIEX Event

Singaraja- International level achievements were again achieved by students of Universitas Pendidikan Ganesha (Undiksha). This time, it came from the Indonesia International Invention Expo (IIIEX 2022) which took place at Semarang State University on October 15, 2022. The Undiksha team consisting of Kadek Nova Suadnyana from the History Education Study Program, Kadek Desika Natalia from the Biology Study Program, I Komang Andi Suryawan from the Elementary School Teacher Education Study Program (PGSD), Ni Made Rida Divayani from the History Education Study Program, and Kadek Faldy Dwi Surastawan from the D3 Library Study Program managed to bring home a silver medal.

In the event, the Undiksha team competed with 145 teams from 20 countries. The work presented was about the research of Pecalang Segara Pemuteran Village. The team leader, Nova Suadnyana explained that the work was made because it saw the uniqueness of Pemuteran Village which has Pecalang in marine security. In general, usually pecalang in Bali only serve when there is a religious ceremony, while pecalang immediately focuses mainly on protecting sea waters to prevent prohibited actions. "We are trying to study this from the academic side," he said, Wednesday (10/19/2022).

This 5th semester student hopes that through this work, the Bali Provincial Government can make regulations so that each traditional village in Bali forms Pecalang Segara in each Regency / City as an effort to maintain and preserve the beach / sea which is also an attraction for tourists.Furthermore, under the guidance of I Putu Hendra Mas Martayana, S.Pd., M.A., the preparation for participating in this competition took about 2 months, starting from the registration stage, script writing, to the competition. Although there were obstacles that made him and his team unable to take part in the competition offline, he admitted that it paid off with the awards that were won.

Vice Rector for Student Affairs, Public Relations, and Alumni, Prof. Dr. I Wayan Suastra, M.Pd., expressed his appreciation for this achievement. He hopes that this achievement can continue to increase students' motivation to compete. "We at the leadership are also proud of this achievement. Hopefully more students will succeed in achieving achievements, "he said. (HMS)
